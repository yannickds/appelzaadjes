# About

Ada Lovelace is widely regarded as one of the earliest computer programmers.
She collaborated with Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine and wrote
extensive notes describing how the machine could perform calculations beyond
simple arithmetic.

## Early Life

Born in London, she was the daughter of the poet Lord Byron and Annabella
Milbanke. From a young age she showed an aptitude for mathematics.

## Career

Her notes on the Analytical Engine included what is often considered the first
published computer algorithm intended for implementation by a machine.

## Legacy

Today she is remembered as a pioneer in computing, and the programming
language **Ada** was named in her honor.

## Timeline

- **1815** — Born in London.
- **1833** — Met Charles Babbage.
- **1843** — Published notes on the Analytical Engine.
- **1852** — Died at age 36.

## Interesting Facts

- Considered one of the first computer programmers.
- Envisioned computers manipulating symbols as well as numbers.
- Ada Lovelace Day is celebrated annually to recognize women in STEM.
